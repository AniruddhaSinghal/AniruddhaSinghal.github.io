Section 03

The power of transformation

Not all curves are equal. Linearising data through transformation is one of the most powerful techniques in the analyst's toolkit.

Raw Exponential Data y = a·eᵇˣ + noise
A straight line cannot fit this curve well. The residuals would be enormous.
Log-Transformed Data ln(y) = ln(a) + bx
After taking log(y), the data becomes linear. A straight line fits perfectly.

Section 04

When one variable isn't enough

Real phenomena depend on multiple inputs. Multivariable regression extends the line to a plane — and beyond.

3D Regression Plane · ŷ = β₀ + β₁x₁ + β₂x₂
Plane Coefficients
β₀ (intercept)2.0
β₁ (slope for x₁)1.0
β₂ (slope for x₂)1.0
ŷ = 2.0 + 1.0·x₁ + 1.0·x₂
Linear Dependence
Each output ŷ is a linear combination of inputs. Changing β₁ tilts the plane along x₁. Changing β₂ tilts it along x₂. The intercept β₀ shifts it up or down.
Why linearity matters: The model is interpretable. A unit increase in x₁ always yields exactly β₁ increase in ŷ — regardless of the values of other variables.
