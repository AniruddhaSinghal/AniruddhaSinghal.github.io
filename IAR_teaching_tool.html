The Bullwhip Effect
A small demand ripple at the retail end becomes a violent production swing upstream. Longer production cycles = bigger swings = more excess inventory. Robotics shrinks the cycle, dampening the whip.

ABC-XYZ Diagnostic
Drag inventory items into the matrix to classify them. The colour shows their IAR applicability — how much of that inventory is agility-addressable vs. genuinely required.
A/B/C = Pareto value ranking. A items = top 80% of inventory value.

X/Y/Z = Demand predictability. X = stable (CoV <0.5), Z = volatile (CoV >1.0).

The IAR zone = high-value items with predictable demand (AX, AY, BX). These are held in excess purely because changeover is expensive.
Green cells = high IAR opportunity. Robots directly reduce the inventory buffer here via the EOQ mechanism.

Red cells = genuine safety stock. Not IAR-addressable. Demand is too volatile or value too high.
